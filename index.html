Labs Homepage

Welcome to the homepage hosting the Godot labs created for the course HRY taught at FEE CTU. This course covers topics needed to create video games in the Godot Engine as an alternative to the Unity course used. Topics include: basics of the engine, player and camera controls, 2D/3D platformers, artificial intelligence in games (not LLMs), animations, physics, UI, audio a visual effects.

01 - Basics

Intro to Godot: engine user interface, scenes, nodes, GDScript, and creating simple objects.

Basics needed for all next labs Open lab

02 - Player Controller and Camera

Input, movement, jump, double jump, collisions, and a basic smooth third-person camera.

Two player controllers and Camera Open lab

03 - 2D Platformer

Tilemaps, spritesheets, 2D player controller, sprites, and 2D level design.

Example 2D game Open lab

04 - 2D Platformer in 3D

Implementation of 2D platformer in 3D using Viewports.

2D platformer in 3D Open lab

05 - AI: NavMesh & FSM

Navigation: NavMesh, pathfinding.

Enemy behavior: FSM (finite-state machine) implementation.

Enemy AI and Navigation Open lab

06 - AI: Behavior Trees a Steering

Navigation: Steering, seek, pursue, CollisionAvoidance.

Enemy Behavior: Beehave plugin, design Behavior Trees (BT).

Advanced Enemy AI and Navigation Open lab

07 - Animation & Physics

Animation: Animation tree, animation blending, skeletal/procedural/in-engine animations.

Physics: Rigidbodies, physical properties of bodies, RigidBody3D and CharacterBody3D interaction.

Player animations and Physics interactions. Open lab

08 - UI

User interface: Heads-up display (HUD) with a HP bar and a Collectibles counter. Discussion and example of diegetic elements. Main menu with saving/loading.

Functional UI HUD and Main Menu Open lab

09 - Audio

Overview of all kinds of audio playing using simple nodes and an AudioManager.

Playing SFX and Music Open lab

10 - VFX

Creating visual effects using shaders (Fullscreen recolor), visual shaders (Player Hologram), particle emitters, noise, and much more.

Adding game juice Open lab

Bonus materials on all sorts of topics. Recommended for everyone to check out.
